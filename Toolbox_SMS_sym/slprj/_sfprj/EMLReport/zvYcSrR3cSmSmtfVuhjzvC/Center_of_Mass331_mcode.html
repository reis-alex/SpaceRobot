1function r_com = Center_of_Mass(r0,rL,robot)
 2% Computes the center-of-mass (CoM) of the system.
 3%
 4% r_com = Center_of_Mass(r0,rL,robot)
 5%
 6% :parameters: 
 7%   * r0 -- Position of the base-link, projected in the inertial CCS -- [3x1].
 8%   * rL -- Positions of the links, projected in the inertial CCS -- as a [3xn] matrix.
 9%   * robot -- Robot model (see :doc:`/Tutorial_Robot`).
10%
11% :return:
12%   * r_com -- Location of the center-of-mass, projected in the inertial CCS  -- [3x1].
13%
14% Use :func:`src.kinematics_dynamics.Kinematics` to compute ``rL``.
15%
16% This function can also be used to compute the velocity/acceleration of the center-of-mass.
17% To do it use as paremeters the velocities ``r0dot,rLdot`` or acceleration ``r0ddot,rLddot`` and you will get the CoM  velocity ``rcomdot`` or acceleration ``rcomddot``.
18%
19% See also: :func:`src.kinematics_dynamics.Kinematics`

38%=== CODE ===%
39
40%Initialize total mass and total mass * distance
41mass_total=robot.base_link.mass;
42mass_r=r0*robot.base_link.mass;
43
44%Add contributions of links
45for i=1:robot.n_links_joints
46    mass_total=mass_total+robot.links(i).mass;
47    mass_r = mass_r+rL(1:3,i).*robot.links(i).mass; 
48end
49
50%Compute center-of-mass
51r_com=mass_r./mass_total;
52
53end
